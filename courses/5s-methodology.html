5S Workplace Organisation

Most organisations attempt 5S and abandon it within months. This program teaches not just the five steps — but the implementation logic, the sustainability mechanisms, and the mindset shift that separates a 5S initiative that lasts from one that fades after the first audit. Built from direct application in both industrial and finance back-office environments.

Program overview

What this program covers.

5S is the most misunderstood tool in the Lean toolkit. Most implementations stop at Sort and Set in Order — the visible, easy part. The real value comes from Standardise and Sustain, which require cultural commitment and management systems that most teams have never built. This program covers all five pillars in depth, with a specific focus on making 5S stick in finance, administrative, and back-office environments where "workplace organisation" looks very different from a factory floor.

You will leave with a complete 5S implementation roadmap, audit frameworks, visual management standards, and the team engagement tools needed to sustain the gains long after the initial rollout.

The five pillars
1S
Sort
Remove everything that is not needed. Apply the Red Tag method.
2S
Set in Order
A place for everything, everything in its place. Visual management.
3S
Shine
Clean to inspect. Detect problems through daily discipline.
4S
Standardise
Create the systems that make the first 3S self-sustaining.
5S
Sustain
Build the culture and audit systems that prevent regression.

Program modules

01 Why Most 5S Implementations Fail — and the Framework That Works ~70 min
The origin of 5S: Toyota Production System and the concept of visual workplace
Why 5S fades: the three most common implementation failures
5S in service and finance environments vs. manufacturing — the key differences
The full 5S implementation roadmap: from assessment to Sustain
Getting leadership buy-in: the business case for 5S in back-office settings
02 Sort (1S) — Eliminating What Doesn't Belong ~65 min
Identifying necessary vs. unnecessary items: the decision matrix
The Red Tag method: how to run a Sort event step by step
Applying Sort to digital environments: files, emails, shared drives, and inboxes
Dealing with resistance: "we might need it one day" and other common objections
Sort event templates and checklists (ready to use)
03 Set in Order & Shine (2S + 3S) — Visual Workplace and Inspection Through Cleaning ~80 min
The principle of visual management: make the normal obvious, make the abnormal visible
Designing workspace layouts: physical and digital "a place for everything"
Labelling, colour coding, and shadow boarding for office environments
Digital Set in Order: folder structures, file naming conventions, shared drive standards
Shine as inspection: using the cleaning routine to detect problems before they escalate
Daily 5-minute Shine routines that teams will actually maintain
04 Standardise (4S) — Building Systems That Self-Sustain ~75 min
What standardisation means in 5S — and why it is the hardest pillar to achieve
Writing 5S standards that people can actually follow: format, language, visuals
One-Point Lessons (OPL): the tool for capturing and sharing best practices
Designing the 5S zone map and assigning ownership
Integrating 5S standards into onboarding and SOPs
05 Sustain (5S) — Audit Systems, Culture, and Long-Term Governance ~80 min
The Sustain paradox: why improvement without discipline always regresses
Designing a 5S audit system: scoring rubrics, frequency, and escalation paths
The 5S board: visual tracking of scores, trends, and team accountability
Leadership behaviours that reinforce 5S: the management routine toolkit
Connecting 5S to KPIs: quality, productivity, and error rate metrics
Running a 5S maturity assessment: where your team is and where to go next
