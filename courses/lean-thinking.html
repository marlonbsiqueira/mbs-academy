Program overview

What this program covers.

Lean Thinking is the intellectual foundation beneath every Lean tool — 5S, Kaizen, Value Stream Mapping, DMAIC. Without it, those tools are just activities. With it, they become a system. This program teaches the five principles of Lean Thinking as defined by Womack and Jones, then goes beyond theory to show how they actually manifest — and fail — in finance and service environments that look nothing like the Toyota factory where they originated.

This is not a history of Toyota. It is a practical framework for building a Lean mindset that makes every tool you already know — or will learn — significantly more effective. Ideal as a standalone foundation course or as preparation before diving into Kaizen facilitation, Six Sigma, or process redesign projects.

The five Lean principles
1
Value
Define what the customer actually values — not what we assume.
2
Value Stream
Map the full flow of activities. Expose every step that adds no value.
3
Flow
Make value-adding steps flow without interruption, defects, or batching.
4
Pull
Produce only what the next step needs, when it needs it.
5
Perfection
Pursue continuous improvement relentlessly — there is no finish line.
