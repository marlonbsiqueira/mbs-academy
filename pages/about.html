Background

From operational finance to transformation architecture.

I started my career at the intersection of operational finance and process improvement — building Lean Six Sigma capability at Vale S.A., one of the world's largest mining companies, and then moving into financial strategy at ALE Combustíveis, where I led cost optimisation and Balanced Scorecard deployment across multiple business units.

The inflection point came when I joined multinational SSC/GBS environments and realised that the gap in most finance functions was not a lack of talent — it was a structural problem. Finance teams were spending 60–80% of their time collecting and reconciling data, and maybe 20% on the analysis that actually drives decisions. Fixing that required integrating three things most organisations keep separate: financial rigour, process discipline, and technical execution.

That integration is what I have spent the last 12+ years building — at Meridian, at Fundação Renova, and currently at Stellantis, where I lead SAP S/4HANA transformation and intelligent automation across 15+ European legal entities.

The training programs I deliver here are not summaries of what I've read. They are distillations of what I've built and what I've seen work — and fail — in real environments with real constraints and real consequences.
